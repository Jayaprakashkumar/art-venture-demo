COLLECTOR TAKEAWAY

Katz’s 2018 yearly auction sale marks up for 17% its career auction sale since 1984.

High potential to surpass the last annual auction sale peak (2014) and investors expectations of reduced turbulence in sell-through rate as a result of the increased supply on the secondary market and media exposure from commercial brands.

Strong investor confidence on Katz’s flower paintings from 1984-2014. However, bearish investment on the recent supply on the secondary market after the supply break.

Katz achieved great public prominence in the 1980s. He is well known for his large-scale paintings with bold simplicity and intense colours.

Portrait and natural landscape have been Katz’s two primary subjects since the beginning of his career.

The Colby College Museum of Art opened a 10,000-square-foot wing dedicated to Katz that features more than 400 oil paintings, collages, and prints donated by the artist in 1996. It is one of the few wings of a museum in the United States devoted solely to the work of a single living artist.
