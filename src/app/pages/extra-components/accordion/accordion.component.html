From 2017 to 2018, Alex Katz’s auction sales increased by 200%. This unprecedented growth is due to the increased supply and recognition of his best-selling series, Ada, and the positive reception of Coca-Cola Girls and Calvin Klein Girls.

Overall, Alex Katz had a remarkable career art auction sale performance, with total sales summing to $42,971,759 and a sell-through rate of 86%, indicating high demand for his work. Moreover, the 2018 sale ($7,277,422) generated 17% of his career total sales over 34 years, 50 sold artworks with an average hammer price of $145,548.44 . His best-selling “Ada” and “Flower” series, have totalled to $9.32M, which constitutes approximately one fourth of his total sales. In 2017 and 2018, multiple major media agencies such as The New Yorker, New York Times, The Guardian and Financial Times featured interviews Katz in anticipation of his book Looking at Art with Alex Katz as well as to promote his gallery exhibitions for Coca-Cola Girls and Calvin Klein Girls. The increase in media coverage of Alex Katz in the past two years likely had a positive impact on his art market performance. Therefore, it is recommended to invest in Katz’s “Ada” or “Flower” series works produced between (2000-2010) on the US art market auction market through Christie’s and Sotheby’s, as three of the top five highest-sold paintings were part of these two series in recent years.
