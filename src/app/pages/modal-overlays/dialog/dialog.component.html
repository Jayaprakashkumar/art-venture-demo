Alex Katz’s artwork shows lower volatility in recent years compared to auction houses’ estimation pricing and negative correlation with stocks and gold, making a great addition to investors’ diversified portfolio.

HAMMER PRICE VS. LOW ESTIMATE PRICE

In the beginning of his auction art market career from 1984-2004, Alex Katz’s yearly average artwork’s hammer price was consistently sold around 5% above the auction houses’ low estimate price

From 2005-2007, on average, the hammer price was at 150% above the low-estimate line, where it reached its highest peak in 2007 at around 187% beyond the low estimate price.In 2008, the auction houses’ estimation was under the low-estimate line at around 50%. In 2009-2018, the average hammer-low proportion was at ±5% above the auction houses’ estimation.

Through the graph we observe that Alex’s price/cm² change has a standard deviation of 0.60 from the mean of 25.70%. This is only slightly higher compared to the S&P, Gold and FTSE which have a standard deviation of 0.17, 0.16 and 0.14 from their mean of 3.90%, 8.25% and 2.50% respectively. From 2001 to 2009, the volatility in Alex’s ratio of price to cm² is 0.78. This majorly reduces by 56% to 0.34 in 2010 to 2018. This trend showcases the steadiness and reduction in volatility that Alex’s works have and could continue to face. This will make it a more stable instrument to invest in.
