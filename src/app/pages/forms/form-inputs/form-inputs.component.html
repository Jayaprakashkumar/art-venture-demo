HAMMER PRICE EVOLUTION

Alex Katz’s paintings’ market price are consistently evolving throughout the years on the secondary market.

Alex Katz debuted his art market auction sale in 1984. In the early stages of his auction career, his average volume of supply was at 10 paintings per year with approximately 93% unsold. In 2001, Katz ’s total auction sales ($929,500) rose past previous years’ performance by $794,500. This growth was due to the sale of his artwork called Self-Portrait (Cigarette), which sold for $600,000, ranking the third highest price among his best-sold paintings. In 2007, Katz’ total auction sales of $2,110,087 surpassed the $2M-line and outperformed the annual top-estimate line by $591,358 due to the sale of Red Tulips for $580,000, which ranked fourth among his best-sold paintings.
