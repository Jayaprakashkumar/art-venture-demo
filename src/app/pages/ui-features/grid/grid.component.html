HAMMER PRICE AND SELL THROUGH RATE, ADA
High potential to surpass the last annual auction sale peak (2014) and investors expectations of reduced turbulence in sell-through rate as a result of the increased supply on the secondary market.

Katz’s “Ada” series’ art market auction sale totalled to $5,935,751, above the low estimate price ($5,302,192) and below the high estimate price ($7,700,053). In 2001, the “Ada” series’ total sale was nine times higher than its previous year’s sale because of “Blue Umbrella #2” that was sold for $600K (ranked third in highest-sold works). Throughout his career, this series’ annual hammer price fluctuates between the auction house’s estimation prices every other year. After 2014, a positive outlook in the growth of this series’ sale performance shows the yearly hammer price is consistently increasing. Despite the low sell-through rates in 2001 and 2008, each respectively at 33% and 25%, around 91% of the “Ada” series’ work was sold on the auction market, demonstrating a high investor demand. His annual average hammer price is around 1.35 times beyond the low estimate price. This series’s most supplied price range is between $20-40K, where the average price/cm² is at $18.77.

According to the two graphs, we can see this series’ strong increase in supply and in total sales after 2017.
